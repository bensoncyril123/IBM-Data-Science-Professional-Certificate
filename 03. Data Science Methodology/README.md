## 📄 About
This course introduces structured frameworks for approaching data science problems systematically. It focuses on the IBM Foundational Methodology and the industry-standard CRISP-DM framework — providing a repeatable, disciplined process for moving from a business problem to a deployable solution.

---

## 📑 Topics Covered
- The IBM Foundational Methodology for Data Science (10 stages)
- CRISP-DM: Business Understanding → Data Understanding → Data Preparation → Modelling → Evaluation → Deployment
- Problem formulation: translating business questions into analytical questions
- Data requirements and collection strategies
- Data understanding and exploratory analysis
- Modelling approaches and model selection
- Evaluation, feedback loops, and deployment considerations

---

## 🔑 Key Skills Gained
| Skill | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| CRISP-DM | Applying the industry-standard data science process framework |
| Problem Framing | Translating business problems into structured analytical questions |
| Iterative Thinking | Understanding the iterative nature of data science projects |
| Model Evaluation | Evaluating models against business objectives, not just metrics |

---

## 💡 Key Takeaway
> *Methodology is what separates data science from ad hoc analysis. Starting with a clearly defined business question and following a structured process ensures that the work is focused, reproducible, and solves the right problem. A model that answers the wrong question is worthless — no matter how accurate it is.*
