# Course 3 — Data Science Methodology

## 📄 Summary
This course introduces structured frameworks for approaching data science problems systematically. It focuses on the IBM Foundational Methodology and the industry-standard CRISP-DM (Cross-Industry Standard Process for Data Mining) framework — providing a repeatable, disciplined process for moving from business problem to deployable solution.

## 📑 Main Topics
- The IBM Foundational Methodology for Data Science (10 stages)
- CRISP-DM: Business Understanding → Data Understanding → Data Preparation → Modelling → Evaluation → Deployment
- Problem formulation: translating business questions into analytical questions
- Data requirements and data collection strategies
- Data understanding and exploratory analysis
- Modelling approaches and model selection
- Evaluation, feedback loops, and deployment considerations

## 🔑 Key Skills Learned
- Applying the CRISP-DM framework to real-world data science problems
- Formulating the right analytical question from a business problem
- Understanding the iterative nature of data science projects
- Evaluating models against business objectives — not just statistical metrics
- Structuring end-to-end data science workflows

## 💡 My Takeaway
Methodology is what separates data science from ad hoc analysis. Starting with a clearly defined business question and following a structured process ensures that the work is focused, reproducible, and actually solves the right problem. A model that answers the wrong question is worthless — no matter how accurate it is.
